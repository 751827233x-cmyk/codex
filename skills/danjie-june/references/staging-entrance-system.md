# 开场与入场调度系统

Use this reference with the Danjie June storyboard rules whenever a scene begins, a new character appears, a hidden character is revealed, a group enters, or a script starts with characters already in confrontation. The goal is to avoid characters "hard-cutting" into place without a professional staging process.

## Core Principle

Every scene opening and every meaningful character appearance must have a visible staging process before the conflict fully fires. The process may be brief, but it must answer:

- Where does the viewer first locate the space?
- How does the character enter, become visible, or get revealed?
- Where does the character settle in the current frame/aspect ratio?
- Who notices or reacts first?
- How does the new position join the existing axis without flipping screen direction?

Do not add new plot, new dialogue, new clothing, or unsupported props. Add only physical process, reaction, space, sound, and camera logic that naturally fits the source.

## Detection Pass

Before drafting, classify each beat:

- **Scene opening**: first beat of a new location, time, or physical space.
- **Mid-conflict opening**: source begins with characters already arguing, fighting, bargaining, kneeling, hiding, or confronting each other.
- **Explicit entrance**: words like appears, enters, walks in, rushes in, falls, lands, returns, pushes door, turns up, arrives, catches up, comes over.
- **Revealed presence**: a character was off-screen, hidden, behind a door, in shadow, outside the frame, or newly noticed.
- **Power entrance**: a new arrival changes the pressure relationship, threat level, rescue possibility, or who controls the scene.
- **Exit or attempted exit**: a character leaves, turns away, retreats, runs, disappears, or is blocked.

Reflect the result in `本段生成规划` under `开场/入场调度`.

## Scene Opening Patterns

Choose the opening pattern based on the source:

- **Atmosphere-to-action**: start with a short full/relationship shot, then move into the physical action already underway. Use when the scene has a clear place and mood.
- **Action-in-progress**: if the script starts mid-argument or mid-fight, show the last half-second of pre-dialogue physical behavior before the first line: a step stops, a hand tightens, someone leans in, someone refuses to retreat.
- **Discovery opening**: if a secret, body, object, or hidden person matters, open with the watcher or clue, then reveal the subject.
- **Power-position opening**: if hierarchy matters, first show who is higher/lower, nearer/farther, seated/standing, protected/exposed, surrounded/isolated.
- **Silent pressure opening**: for tension scenes, allow a 2秒 silent shot before dialogue where breath, stillness, eye-line, or distance creates pressure.

New scenes still need a full shot, but a full shot is not enough. It must contain a staging purpose: who is already in motion, who refuses to move, who controls the space, or what risk is present.

## Entrance Patterns

Choose an entrance pattern for any new or newly revealed character:

- **Threshold entrance**: character crosses a doorway, forest edge, curtain edge, corridor mouth, room boundary, or frame edge. Show threshold -> step/arrival -> settle.
- **Reveal by reaction**: existing character notices first; camera follows eye-line to the arrival. Use when surprise or fear matters.
- **Reveal by sound**: footsteps, fabric, breath, weapon, thunder, door, branch, water, or magic sound arrives before the character is fully seen.
- **Reveal by light or movement**: a shadow, edge light, flicker, dust, ripple, or movement announces the character before the full body appears.
- **Drop/landing/teleport appearance**: show arrival cue -> physical landing or light settling -> body balance -> existing characters react -> new axis integrated.
- **Group entrance**: establish the leader first, then place companions relative to the leader without breaking existing left/right assignments.
- **Silent already-present reveal**: if a character was present but unnoticed, reveal their position with a rack focus, pan, or foreground/background relationship shot.
- **Blocked exit**: for attempted exits, show intention -> path -> obstruction/reaction -> revised state.

## Five-Step Staging Chain

For scene openings and entrances, include as many of these steps as the beat needs:

1. **Cue**: sound, movement, light, eye-line shift, body tension, or spatial clue.
2. **Approach or reveal**: crossing frame edge, emerging from background, landing, stepping into the current-frame safe zone, or being discovered by camera movement.
3. **Settle**: the character stops, balances, lowers a hand, turns, looks up, holds a posture, or claims a lane.
4. **Reaction**: at least one existing character reacts with eye-line, breath, posture, silence, or a small movement.
5. **Integration**: update the axis, screen side, depth layer, distance, and who now controls the pressure.

Do not always use all five as separate shots. For simple entrances, combine them into one or two dense shots. For power entrances, use two to four shots if needed.

## Opening Before First Dialogue

If a scene starts with a line of dialogue, the first spoken line should usually be preceded by a visual lead-in unless the user explicitly asks for immediate dialogue.

Use one of these:

- Last beat of an ongoing action: someone takes half a step, stops, refuses to retreat, or turns.
- Silent pressure: both sides hold distance, one person watches, the other decides to speak.
- Spatial reveal: camera shows the relationship that makes the line dangerous.
- Listening beat: one character hears, looks, or notices before speaking.

The lead-in should be short: normally 2秒, sometimes 3秒 for complex space. It must not add new plot information beyond what the scene already implies.

## Entry And Axis Rules

- Every entrance must name the entry direction and final screen lane.
- A new character joins the existing axis as left, center, right, foreground, midground, or background. Do not let their arrival flip existing screen direction.
- If the arrival creates a new dominant axis, write the reason in the continuity ledger and show a visible axis reset shot.
- If the arrival is behind, above, below, hidden, or far away, preserve that relation. Do not pull them into the foreground for convenience.
- If the entry route is blocked or watched, show the watcher or obstruction as part of the staging chain.

## Dialogue Timing During Entrances

- Do not let a newly arrived character speak before the viewer understands where they are, unless surprise disorientation is the intended effect.
- For sudden entrances, allow the first line after the body settles or after existing characters react.
- If the script has an immediate shout during entry, fuse the line with the motion: breath before the shout, body still moving, final word lands as the character stops.
- Never place dialogue in a full shot unless the full shot is only spatial and silent under Danjie rules. Move the line into the next medium or medium-close shot.

## Exit And Interrupted Exit

Apply the same staging logic to exits:

- **Exit intention**: glance to path, body turns, foot shifts, hand moves, or speech covers retreat.
- **Route confirmation**: show where the path is in the frame and who can block it.
- **Exit movement**: character moves along the established screen direction.
- **Reaction/block**: someone watches, stops them, or lets them go.
- **State refresh**: after exit, update who remains, where pressure lands, and whether the absent character still affects the scene.

## Shot Formula Add-On

For opening/entrance shots, add a natural staging phrase to the usual shot line:

```text
2秒 中景 / 缓慢推进 / 入口侧同轴；镜头先给出入场线索，角色从画面右后进入并在中景右侧停稳，既有人先转头反应，随后空间关系更新为A左、B中、C右；环境声/动作声；[光效]
```

Do not write labels like `入场五步法` in the final shot. Write the process as visible action.

## Segment Planning Field

In `本段生成规划`, include:

```text
- 开场/入场调度：场景开场 / 中途入场 / 揭示在场 / 退场 / 无；采用XX模式，入场线索为XX，落位为XX，反应由XX承担。
```

If there is no opening or entrance, write `无；承接上一段连续动作`.

## Self-Check

Reject and revise if:

- A new scene begins with people already speaking or fighting without any spatial or behavioral lead-in.
- A new character appears already standing in final position with no cue, arrival, settle, or reaction.
- A power-changing entrance lacks existing-character reaction.
- A character enters from an unspecified direction or lands in an unspecified lane.
- An entrance flips established left/right screen direction without a visible reset.
- A character speaks before the shot establishes where they are, unless the source explicitly demands disorienting interruption.
- An exit happens without path, direction, reaction, and final state refresh.
